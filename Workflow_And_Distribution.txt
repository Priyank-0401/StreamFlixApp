================================================================
  SECTION 1 — COMPLETE APPLICATION WORKFLOW
================================================================

Below is the full end-to-end workflow of the application.
Every step mentions which user story it connects to.


----------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 1: SYSTEM SETUP (Admin sets up before customers arrive)
----------------------------------------------------------------

Step 1: Admin logs into the Admin Console.
  System authenticates via OAuth2/JWT, checks role = ADMIN.
  -> Connects to US 10 (RBAC)

Step 2: Admin creates Products (e.g., "StreamMax Platform").
  Under each product, Admin creates Plans:
    - Basic Plan:  Rs.299/month, 7-day trial, no setup fee
    - Pro Plan:    Rs.599/month, 14-day trial, Rs.99 setup fee
    - Annual Plan: Rs.4999/year, no trial, no setup fee
  -> Connects to US 03 (Product/Plan/Pricing Configuration)

Step 3: Admin sets up Tax Rules:
    - India: 18% GST, exclusive
    - EU:   20% VAT, inclusive
  Admin creates Coupons:
    - "WELCOME20": 20% off, first month only, max 500 uses
    - "FLAT100":   Rs.100 off, recurring 3 months, no limit
  -> Connects to US 04 (Taxes & Discounts)

Step 4: Admin configures Dunning retry strategy:
    - Retry after 1 day, 3 days, 7 days
    - After all retries fail: cancel subscription
  -> Connects to US 09 (Dunning & Retry)

Step 5: Admin configures Notification templates:
    - Renewal reminder at T-7 and T-1 days before billing
    - Receipt email after successful payment
    - Payment failure alert with "Update Card" link
  -> Connects to US 05 (Renewal Reminders)


----------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 2: CUSTOMER ONBOARDING
----------------------------------------------------------------

Step 6: Customer creates an account.
  System stores: name, email, phone, currency, address.
  Customer logs in, JWT token issued, role = CUSTOMER.
  -> Connects to US 10 (RBAC - customer role)

Step 7: Customer browses the Product Catalog.
  Sees available plans with prices, features, trial info.
  -> Connects to US 03 (Plan listing)

Step 8: Customer selects a Plan (e.g., Pro Plan Rs.599/month
  with 14-day trial).
  Optionally adds Add-ons.
  Enters payment method (tokenized, no raw card stored).
  -> Connects to US 01 (Subscribe to a Plan)

Step 9: Customer applies coupon code "WELCOME20" at checkout.
  System validates: not expired, usage limit OK, plan OK.
  Total updates: Rs.599 -> Rs.479.20 + 18% GST = Rs.565.46
  -> Connects to US 14 (Apply Coupon at Checkout)

Step 10: System creates the Subscription:
  - Plan has trial? -> status = TRIALING, no charge now
  - No trial?       -> status = ACTIVE, invoice generated
  - Setup fee?      -> charge setup fee immediately
  On payment success -> send receipt email
  On payment failure -> mark past_due, schedule retry
  -> Connects to US 01 + US 02


----------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 3: SUBSCRIPTION LIFECYCLE (Day-to-day usage)
----------------------------------------------------------------

Step 11: 7 days before renewal, system sends reminder:
  "Your Pro Plan renews on 15-May-2026.
   Amount: Rs.565.46. Manage subscription: [link]"
  1 day before, another reminder is sent.
  -> Connects to US 05 (Renewal Reminders)

Step 12: Customer upgrades from Pro (Rs.599) to Annual
  (Rs.4999).
  System calculates proration:
    - 20 days remaining on Pro -> credit Rs.399.33
    - 20 days on Annual -> charge Rs.273.89
    - Net charge: Rs.200 (charged now or next invoice)
  -> Connects to US 06 (Upgrade/Downgrade with Proration)

Step 13: Customer requests to pause subscription.
  Support agent sets pause: May 20 -> June 10.
  Status = PAUSED. Billing engine skips paused subs.
  On June 10, status auto-reverts to ACTIVE.
  -> Connects to US 12 (Pause/Resume)

Step 14: Customer manages notification preferences:
  Turns off SMS marketing. Keeps email receipts ON.
  Mandatory alerts (payment failed) cannot be turned off.
  -> Connects to US 15 (Notification Preferences)


----------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 4: AUTOMATED BILLING (Runs daily at 2 AM via Cron)
----------------------------------------------------------------

Step 15: Billing engine wakes up (Cron job).
  Finds all ACTIVE subscriptions expiring tomorrow.
  Skips PAUSED or already-invoiced subs (idempotency).
  -> Connects to US 02 (Automated Billing Cycles)

Step 16: For each due subscription:
  a) Fetch plan price, tax rate, active coupon
  b) Generate Invoice with line items:
       Line 1: "Pro Plan - Monthly"     Rs.599.00
       Line 2: "Discount (WELCOME20)"  -Rs.119.80
       Line 3: "GST 18%"               +Rs.86.26
       Total:                           Rs.565.46
  c) Attempt payment via mock gateway
  d) SUCCESS -> invoice = PAID, send receipt email
  e) FAILURE -> invoice = PAYMENT_FAILED, enter dunning
  -> Connects to US 02 + US 04 + US 11

Step 17: Customer views invoice history in their portal.
  Sees: Invoice #1042 | May 2026 | Rs.565.46 | PAID
  Clicks [Download PDF] -> formatted PDF with line items.
  -> Connects to US 11 (Invoice History & PDF)


----------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 5: PAYMENT FAILURE & DUNNING
----------------------------------------------------------------

Step 18: Payment attempt fails (card expired, etc.).
  System marks subscription as PAST_DUE.
  Dunning engine schedules retries:
    Day 1: Retry -> fails -> "update your card" email
    Day 3: Retry -> fails -> another reminder
    Day 7: Retry -> fails -> MAX RETRIES EXHAUSTED
  -> Connects to US 09 (Dunning & Retry)

Step 19: After all retries exhausted:
  System cancels subscription (or on_hold - configurable).
  Sends: "Subscription canceled due to payment failure."
  -> Connects to US 09 + US 05


----------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 6: CUSTOMER SUPPORT
----------------------------------------------------------------

Step 20: Customer calls support about a wrong charge.
  Support agent logs in (role = SUPPORT).
  Looks up customer -> subscription history, invoices.
  -> Connects to US 10 (RBAC - support role)

Step 21: Support agent issues a full refund of Rs.565.46.
  System processes refund via mock gateway.
  Auto-creates a Credit Note linked to original invoice.
  Audit log records: who, amount, reason, timestamp.
  -> Connects to US 08 (Refunds & Credit Notes)


----------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 7: FINANCE & REPORTING
----------------------------------------------------------------

Step 22: Finance Manager logs in (role = FINANCE).
  Dashboard shows real-time KPIs:
    MRR:         Rs.4,52,000
    ARR:         Rs.54,24,000
    ARPU:        Rs.487
    Gross Churn: 3.2%
    Net Churn:   1.8%
    LTV:         Rs.14,610
  Charts show trends over the selected time range.
  -> Connects to US 07 (Revenue Analytics)

Step 23: Finance Manager exports monthly report as CSV.
  Downloads churn report as PDF for board presentation.
  -> Connects to US 07 (Export)

Step 24: Finance Manager checks audit logs:
  Filters by action = "REFUND".
  Sees: Agent X refunded Rs.565.46 for Customer Y
        on 12-May-2026, reason: "Service not used".
  Logs are immutable - cannot be edited or deleted.
  -> Connects to US 13 (Audit Log)

----------------------------------------------------------------
DETAILED EXPLANATION
----------------------------------------------------------------

US 01 — Must Have | SUBSCRIBE TO A PLAN

  As a Customer, I want to subscribe to a plan (with
  optional add-ons) so that I can start using the service.

  What this means:
  - Customer picks a plan (e.g., Basic Rs.299/month)
  - Optionally selects add-ons (e.g., Extra Storage Rs.99)
  - Chooses billing cycle (monthly or yearly)
  - Enters payment details (tokenized, no raw card)
  - Plan has trial? -> starts as TRIALING, no charge
  - No trial? -> starts as ACTIVE, first invoice generated

  Acceptance Criteria:
  - Choose plan, billing cycle, add-ons
  - Enter payment method
  - Trial start/end dates shown when applicable
  - Active on success; past_due on failure

................................................................

US 02 — Must Have | AUTOMATED BILLING

  As a System, I want to run automated billing cycles so
  that subscriptions are renewed on time.

  What this means:
  - A Cron job runs every day (e.g., at 2 AM)
  - Finds subscriptions whose period ends tomorrow
  - Generates invoice with line items, taxes, discounts
  - Attempts payment via mock gateway
  - Success: invoice = PAID, sends receipt email
  - Failure: invoice = FAILED, starts dunning
  - Idempotency keys prevent double-charging

  Acceptance Criteria:
  - Cron detects subs due in next 24 hours
  - Generate invoice -> charge -> update status
  - Success: receipt; Failure: past_due + retry
  - Idempotency key prevents duplicate charges

................................................................

US 03 — Must Have | PRODUCT & PLAN MANAGEMENT

  As an Admin, I want to configure products, plans, and
  pricing to offer flexible subscriptions.

  What this means:
  - Admin creates a Product (e.g., "StreamMax")
  - Under it, creates Plans with different prices/features
  - Each plan has: billing period, price, trial days, fee
  - Price books for different currencies (INR, USD, EUR)
  - Plans can have effective date ranges (future pricing)

  Acceptance Criteria:
  - CRUD for product, plan, add-on, metered component
  - Define price, billing period, trial, setup fee
  - Price books per currency; effective start/end dates

................................................................

US 04 — Must Have | TAXES & DISCOUNTS

  As a Finance Manager, I want taxes and discounts applied
  correctly for compliance and transparency.

  What this means:
  - Tax rules per region (India=18% GST, EU=20% VAT)
  - Tax can be inclusive or exclusive
  - Coupons: percentage (20% off) or fixed (Rs.100 off)
  - Coupons: one-time, recurring N months, or forever
  - Coupons have expiry dates and usage limits
  - Invoices show tax and discount as separate line items

  Acceptance Criteria:
  - Tax rules by region, inclusive/exclusive
  - Coupons: amount/%, one-time/recurring, expiry, limits
  - Invoices show line-item taxes and discounts

................................................................

US 05 — Must Have | RENEWAL REMINDERS

  As a Customer, I want renewal reminders so that I am
  informed before charges.

  What this means:
  - System sends email/SMS reminders before auto-charging
  - Default: 7 days and 1 day before renewal (configurable)
  - Includes: amount, date, link to manage subscription
  - All notifications are logged (who, what, when, status)
  - Respects opt-out preferences for non-mandatory alerts

  Acceptance Criteria:
  - Reminders at T-7 and T-1 days (configurable)
  - Email/SMS with amount, date, manage link (Mock)
  - Notification logs maintained

................................................................

US 06 — Must Have | MID-CYCLE UPGRADE/DOWNGRADE

  As a Customer, I want upgrades/downgrades mid-cycle with
  proration so charges are fair.

  What this means:
  - Customer switches plans mid-billing period
  - System calculates proration:
    Credit for unused days on old plan
    Charge for remaining days on new plan
  - Example: 10 days into 30-day month
    Old plan Rs.300, new plan Rs.600
    Credit: Rs.200, Charge: Rs.400, Net: Rs.200
  - Proration amounts appear on the invoice

  Acceptance Criteria:
  - Proration based on remaining days/usage
  - Charge immediately or at next renewal
  - Proration lines appear on invoice

................................................................

US 07 — Must Have | REVENUE ANALYTICS

  As a Finance Manager, I want revenue analytics so that
  I can track MRR/ARR and churn.

  What this means:
  - Dashboard shows key business metrics:
    MRR  = total monthly income from active subs
    ARR  = MRR x 12
    ARPU = MRR / active customers
    Gross Churn = % customers who canceled this month
    Net Churn   = churn minus revenue from upgrades
    LTV  = avg revenue per customer over lifetime
  - Metrics stored as daily snapshots
  - Filterable by time range, plan, region
  - Export as CSV or PDF

  Acceptance Criteria:
  - KPIs: MRR, ARR, ARPU, Net & Gross Churn, LTV
  - Filters by time range, plan, region
  - Export CSV/PDF

................................................................

US 08 — Must Have | REFUNDS & CREDIT NOTES

  As a Support Agent, I want to issue refunds/credit notes
  so that customer disputes are handled.

  What this means:
  - Full refund (entire amount) or partial refund
  - Must provide a reason (for compliance)
  - System auto-creates Credit Note linked to invoice
  - Revenue reports updated to reflect refund
  - Every refund recorded in audit log

  Acceptance Criteria:
  - Full/partial refund with reason
  - Auto-create credit note; update reports
  - Audit log captured

................................................................

US 09 — Must Have | DUNNING & RETRY

  As an Admin, I want dunning & retry strategies so failed
  payments recover automatically.

  What this means:
  - When payment fails, system retries on a schedule
  - Schedule: retry after 1 day, 3 days, 7 days
  - After each failure: "please update your card" email
  - All retries fail? -> cancel or on_hold (configurable)
  - "Dunning" = the process of recovering failed revenue

  Acceptance Criteria:
  - Retry schedule (e.g., after 1d, 3d, 7d)
  - Card update reminder to customer
  - After max retries: cancel or hold (configurable)

................................................................

US 10 — Must Have | ROLE-BASED ACCESS CONTROL (RBAC)

  As an Admin, I want role-based access so staff only see
  what's relevant to their jobs.

  What this means:
  - 4 roles: Admin, Finance, Support, Customer
  - Admin: manage products, plans, coupons, tax, roles
  - Finance: view invoices, reports, audit logs
            (cannot edit products)
  - Support: look up customers, issue refunds
            (cannot view company revenue)
  - Customer: manage own subscription, own invoices
  - Unauthorized access returns 403 Forbidden

  Acceptance Criteria:
  - Finance cannot edit product catalogs
  - Support cannot view total company MRR/ARR
  - Unauthorized access returns 403
  - Role assignments managed through admin interface

................................................................

US 11 — Must Have | INVOICE HISTORY & PDF DOWNLOAD

  As a Customer, I want to view past invoices and download
  them as PDFs for my records.

  What this means:
  - Customer portal shows paginated list of invoices
  - Each shows: number, date, amount, status (PAID/DUE)
  - Click "Download PDF" for formatted document
  - PDF: company header, customer details, line items,
         taxes, discounts, total, payment status

  Acceptance Criteria:
  - Paginated list of past invoices
  - One-click PDF download with full details
  - Invoices clearly show "Paid" or "Due" status

................................................................

US 12 — Should Have | PAUSE & RESUME SUBSCRIPTION

  As a Support Agent, I want to pause and resume a
  subscription to prevent the customer from churning.

  What this means:
  - Customer requests a pause (vacation, etc.)
  - Set pause start date and auto-resume date
  - While paused: status = PAUSED, billing skips it
  - On resume date: status -> ACTIVE, new period starts
  - No charges during the pause period

  Acceptance Criteria:
  - Pause start date and auto-resume date
  - Billing engine skips paused accounts
  - Status: "paused" -> "active" upon resume

................................................................

US 13 — Must Have | AUDIT LOG

  As a Finance Manager, I want an immutable audit log of
  all monetary actions for compliance.

  What this means:
  - Every financial action logged: refunds, plan changes,
    cancellations, manual adjustments
  - Each entry: who (actor), what (action), old value,
    new value, timestamp
  - Immutable: cannot be edited or deleted
  - Filterable by customer ID, action type, date range

  Acceptance Criteria:
  - Captures Actor, Action, Old/New Value, Timestamp
  - Cannot be edited or deleted from frontend
  - Filterable by Customer ID or Action Type

................................................................

US 14 — Must Have | APPLY COUPON AT CHECKOUT

  As a Customer, I want to apply a coupon code during
  checkout or upgrade to get a discount.

  What this means:
  - Input field for coupon code on checkout page
  - Customer enters code (e.g., "WELCOME20")
  - System validates: expired? limit reached? right plan?
  - Valid: total updates showing the discount
  - Invalid: clear error message (expired, wrong plan)

  Acceptance Criteria:
  - Input field for coupon code on the frontend
  - Backend validates expiry, limits, applicable plans
  - Total dynamically updates to reflect discount

................................................................

US 15 — Should Have | NOTIFICATION PREFERENCES

  As a Customer, I want to manage my notification
  preferences to control what alerts I receive.

  What this means:
  - Toggle switches in Customer Portal:
    Email receipts: ON/OFF
    SMS alerts: ON/OFF
    Renewal reminders: ON/OFF
    Marketing emails: ON/OFF
  - System checks preferences before sending
  - Mandatory alerts cannot be turned off:
    Payment failure, subscription cancellation

  Acceptance Criteria:
  - Toggle switches for Email and SMS channels
  - System respects opt-out flags
  - Mandatory transactional emails cannot be opted out


================================================================
  SECTION 3 — USER STORY DISTRIBUTION
  (STORIES FIRST -> DOMAINS EMERGE)
================================================================

Approach: We start from 15 user stories. We group them by
natural affinity - stories that share the same tables,
business logic, or system flow go to the same person.
The "domain" label is just a name for each group.

DISTRIBUTION PRINCIPLES:
  - Each person gets 2-4 user stories
  - Work balanced by COMPLEXITY, not just story count
  - Stories sharing tables/APIs stay together
  - One team member handles frontend centrally
    (all UI screens, all API integrations)


----------------------------------------------------------------
WORK DISTRIBUTION
----------------------------------------------------------------

PERSON 1 | 2 stories | HEAVY workload
  US 03 — Product/Plan/Pricing CRUD
  US 10 — RBAC & Role Management

  Why together:
    Both share the same tables (Customer, Product, Plan)
    and the same security layer (OAuth2, JWT).
    Auth setup is foundational — all other domains
    depend on it. This person also sets up the API
    Gateway which routes all requests.

  Why HEAVY despite only 2 stories:
    Full OAuth2/JWT setup, RBAC for 4 roles, API Gateway,
    Customer CRUD, Product CRUD, Plan CRUD, Add-on CRUD,
    Price Books = very large scope.

................................................................

PERSON 2 | 3 stories | HEAVY workload
  US 01 — Subscribe to a Plan
  US 06 — Upgrade/Downgrade + Proration
  US 12 — Pause/Resume Subscription

  Why together:
    All 3 operate on the Subscription and
    SubscriptionItem tables. The subscription state
    machine (draft -> trialing -> active -> paused ->
    canceled) is one cohesive unit.

  Why HEAVY:
    Proration math is the most complex business logic
    in the entire system. State machine with 6 states
    and multiple transition rules.

................................................................

PERSON 3 | 3 stories | HEAVY workload
  US 02 — Automated Billing Cycles
  US 09 — Dunning & Retry Strategies
  US 11 — Invoice History & PDF Download

  Why together:
    All 3 revolve around invoices. The billing cron
    generates invoices, dunning retries failed invoice
    payments, and US 11 lets customers view/download
    those invoices.
    Tables: Invoice, InvoiceLineItem, CreditNote,
    DunningSchedule.

  Why HEAVY:
    Cron scheduler setup, dunning state machine, PDF
    generation, idempotency checks.

................................................................

PERSON 4 | 3 stories | MEDIUM-HEAVY workload
  US 04 — Taxes & Discounts
  US 08 — Refunds & Credit Notes
  US 14 — Apply Coupon at Checkout

  Why together:
    All 3 are about money math: calculating tax,
    validating coupons, processing payments/refunds.
    Tables: Payment, TaxRate, Coupon.
    This person owns the mock payment gateway,
    tax engine, and coupon engine.

  Why MEDIUM-HEAVY:
    Idempotent payment processing and tax
    inclusive/exclusive modes are key challenges.

................................................................

PERSON 5 | 4 stories | MEDIUM workload
  US 05 — Renewal Reminders
  US 07 — Revenue Analytics (MRR/Churn)
  US 13 — Audit Log Viewing
  US 15 — Notification Preferences

  Why together:
    All 4 are outputs of the system: sending alerts,
    computing metrics, logging actions, managing prefs.
    Tables: Notification, AuditLog, RevenueSnapshot.

  Why MEDIUM despite 4 stories:
    Notification templates are straightforward.
    Audit log is append-only. Preferences are toggles.
    MRR/ARR calculations are the main weight.


----------------------------------------------------------------
COMPLEXITY BALANCE SUMMARY
----------------------------------------------------------------

  Person 1 | 2 stories | VERY HEAVY
    Full OAuth2/JWT, RBAC 4 roles, Gateway,
    Customer + Product + Plan + Add-on CRUD

  Person 2 | 3 stories | HEAVY
    State machine (6 states), proration math,
    pause/resume logic

  Person 3 | 3 stories | HEAVY
    Cron scheduler, invoice generation,
    dunning retries, PDF generation

  Person 4 | 3 stories | MEDIUM-HEAVY
    Mock payment gateway, tax engine,
    coupon engine, refund processing

  Person 5 | 4 stories | MEDIUM
    Notification templates, MRR/ARR calcs,
    audit log (append-only), preference toggles

  RESULT: Person 1 and 3 have heaviest backend work.
  Person 5 has most stories but lightest individual
  complexity. Person 2 has the most critical business
  logic (proration). This balances out.


----------------------------------------------------------------
FRONTEND OWNERSHIP (CENTRALIZED)
----------------------------------------------------------------

One team member handles ALL frontend development:
  - Builds all 4 portals:
      Customer Portal
      Admin Console
      Finance Dashboard
      Support Console
  - Integrates APIs from every domain into the UI
  - Ensures consistent UX (same layout, components)

All other members work on BACKEND ONLY:
  - They build APIs, business logic, DB queries, tests
  - They deliver:
      Swagger documentation
      Postman collections
      Sample JSON responses
  - Frontend developer uses those API contracts to
    build the corresponding screens

This avoids duplicate work, prevents UI merge conflicts,
and ensures visual consistency across the application.

================================================================
  SECTION 6 — DATABASE SCHEMA
================================================================

All monetary amounts are stored in MINOR UNITS:
  Rs.499.00 is stored as 49900 (paise, not rupees)
  $9.99 is stored as 999 (cents, not dollars)

Currency follows ISO-4217 (INR, USD, EUR, etc.)
All timestamps are stored in UTC.

----------------------------------------------------------------
TABLE 1: User
----------------------------------------------------------------
Purpose: Stores login credentials and role assignments.
         Separate from Customer because not all users are
         customers (Admin, Finance, Support are also users).

  Column          Type           Constraints
  ------          ----           -----------
  id              BIGINT         PK, AUTO_INCREMENT
  email           VARCHAR(255)   UNIQUE, NOT NULL

TABLE 6: Subscription
----------------------------------------------------------------
Purpose: Core entity. Represents a customer's active
         relationship with a plan. Tracks lifecycle state.

  Column                Type      Constraints
  ------                ----      -----------
  id                    BIGINT    PK, AUTO_INCREMENT
  customer_id           BIGINT    FK -> Customer(id)
  plan_id               BIGINT    FK -> Plan(id)
  status                ENUM      NOT NULL
                                  (DRAFT, TRIALING,
                                   ACTIVE, PAST_DUE,
                                   PAUSED, CANCELED)
  start_date            DATE      NOT NULL
  current_period_start  DATE      NOT NULL
  current_period_end    DATE      NOT NULL
  cancel_at_period_end  BOOLEAN   DEFAULT FALSE
  paused_from           DATE      NULLABLE
  paused_to             DATE      NULLABLE
  payment_method_id     BIGINT    FK -> PaymentMethod(id)
  currency              VARCHAR(3) NOT NULL
  created_at            TIMESTAMP NOT NULL
  updated_at            TIMESTAMP NOT NULL

  Notes:
  - status is the state machine:
    DRAFT -> TRIALING -> ACTIVE -> PAST_DUE -> CANCELED
                         ACTIVE <-> PAUSED
  - current_period_start/end define the billing window
  - cancel_at_period_end = true means cancel at renewal
    (not immediately)
  - A customer can have multiple subscriptions

TABLE 9: Invoice
----------------------------------------------------------------
Purpose: A bill generated for a subscription renewal
         or one-time charge. Contains line items, taxes,
         discounts, and total.

  Column            Type          Constraints
  ------            ----          -----------
  id                BIGINT        PK, AUTO_INCREMENT
  invoice_number    VARCHAR(50)   UNIQUE, NOT NULL
  customer_id       BIGINT        FK -> Customer(id)
  subscription_id   BIGINT        FK -> Subscription(id)

  Column           Type          Constraints

TABLE 12: TaxRate
----------------------------------------------------------------
Purpose: Tax rules per region. Used to calculate GST/VAT
         on invoices.

  Column           Type          Constraints
  ------           ----          -----------
  id               BIGINT        PK, AUTO_INCREMENT
  region           VARCHAR(50)   NOT NULL (e.g., "IN")
  name             VARCHAR(100)  NOT NULL (e.g., "GST")
  rate_percent     INT           NOT NULL (e.g., 18)
  inclusive        BOOLEAN       NOT NULL
  effective_from   DATE          NOT NULL
  effective_to     DATE          NULLABLE
  created_at       TIMESTAMP     NOT NULL

  Notes:
  - inclusive = true: price already includes tax
    Rs.599 with 18% inclusive -> tax = Rs.91.37
    base = Rs.507.63
  - inclusive = false: tax added on top
    Rs.599 with 18% exclusive -> tax = Rs.107.82
    total = Rs.706.82
  - effective dates allow tax rate changes

  ------              ----        -----------

TABLE 15: DunningSchedule
----------------------------------------------------------------
Purpose: Tracks retry attempts for failed payments.
         The dunning state machine.

  Column           Type          Constraints
  ------           ----          -----------
  id               BIGINT        PK, AUTO_INCREMENT
  invoice_id       BIGINT        FK -> Invoice(id)
  subscription_id  BIGINT        FK -> Subscription(id)
  attempt_count    INT           DEFAULT 0
  max_attempts     INT           DEFAULT 3
  next_retry_date  DATE          NOT NULL
  status           ENUM          NOT NULL
                                 (PENDING, IN_PROGRESS,
                                  RESOLVED, EXHAUSTED)
  last_attempted   TIMESTAMP     NULLABLE
  created_at       TIMESTAMP     NOT NULL
  updated_at       TIMESTAMP     NOT NULL

  Notes:
  - Created when a payment fails for the first time
  - attempt_count increments with each retry
  - next_retry_date follows the retry schedule:
    attempt 1 -> +1 day
    attempt 2 -> +3 days
    attempt 3 -> +7 days
  - RESOLVED = payment eventually succeeded
  - EXHAUSTED = all retries failed, subscription
    canceled or put on hold

TABLE 18: RevenueSnapshot
----------------------------------------------------------------
Purpose: Daily snapshot of business KPIs for the
         analytics dashboard. One row per day.

  Column              Type      Constraints
  ------              ----      -----------
  id                  BIGINT    PK, AUTO_INCREMENT
  snapshot_date       DATE      UNIQUE, NOT NULL
  mrr_minor           BIGINT    NOT NULL
  arr_minor           BIGINT    NOT NULL
  arpu_minor          BIGINT    NOT NULL
  active_customers    INT       NOT NULL
  new_customers       INT       DEFAULT 0
  churned_customers   INT       DEFAULT 0
  gross_churn_percent DECIMAL   NOT NULL (e.g., 3.20)
  net_churn_percent   DECIMAL   NOT NULL (e.g., 1.80)
  ltv_minor           BIGINT    DEFAULT 0
  total_revenue_minor BIGINT    NOT NULL
  total_refunds_minor BIGINT    DEFAULT 0
  created_at          TIMESTAMP NOT NULL

  Notes:
  - One row per day, captured by a daily cron job
  - mrr_minor = sum of all active subscription prices
    (yearly plans divided by 12)
  - arr_minor = mrr_minor * 12
  - arpu_minor = mrr_minor / active_customers
  - gross_churn = churned / active at start of month
  - Used to plot charts on Finance Dashboard
  - Export API returns arrays of these snapshots
